Problems on Ages


  1. t years ago the age of A was n1 times the age of B, and at present A's age is n2 times that of B, then;
    A's present age =(n1-1/n1-n2)n2 * t
    And, B's present age = (n1-1/n1-n2)*t


  2. The present age of A is n1 times the present age of B. After t years, age of A becomes n2 times the age of B, then;
    A's present age =(n2-1/n1-n2)n2*t
    And, B's present age =(n2-1/n1-n2)*t

  3. The ratio of the present ages of A and B is p: q and after t years, it becomes r: s, then;
    A's present age =(pt(r-s)/ps-qr)
    And, B's present age =(qt(r-s)/ps-qr)


Sample Questions

  1. A mother is twice as old as her son. If 20 years ago, the age of the mother was 10 times the age of the son, what is the present age of the mother?

    1. 38
    2. 40
    3. 43
    4. 45

  2. Ten years ago, the sum of ages of a father and his son was 34 years. If the ratio of present ages of the father and son is 7:2, find the present age of the son.
    1. 8
    2. 10
    3. 11
    4. 12
